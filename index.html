Table A.1 Reconstruction error results on the Beethoven dataset. The perceptual metric (\( \|S_K x_i - S_K(G(z_i))\|_1 \)) improves over the mean-square error (MSE) metric (\( \|x_i - G(z_i)\|_2^2 \)). Further, adding the moment-matching term during training improves the reconstruction results and the generalization.

Qualitative results

We present additional reconstruction results on the NSynth and Beethoven datasets.






Figure A.1 Comparison of the reconstruction with the mean-square error metric and the perceptual metric on the testing part of the Beethoven dataset. Each line corresponds to a different example. The left column is the original, the middle column MSE and the right column the perceptual metric. Notice the clear inability of the MSE network to generalize.







Figure A.2 Comparison of the reconstruction with and without the moment matching term, on the testing part of the Beethoven dataset. Each line corresponds to a different example. The left column is the original, the middle column is the reconstruction from a network trained without the moment matching term and the right column the reconstruction from a network trained with the moment matching term. Notice the clear improvement in quality when adding the moment matching term.
